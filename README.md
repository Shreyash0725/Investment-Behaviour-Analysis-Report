# 📊 Investment Behavior Analysis Dashboard

This Power BI project provides a comprehensive analysis of investor behavior, financial goals, risk preferences, and decision-making patterns. The dashboard enables interactive exploration of demographic trends, investment distribution, duration patterns, and influencing factors to support data-driven financial insights.

---

## 📊 Key Features

### 💰 Investment Distribution
Track overall allocation across Mutual Funds, Equity, Fixed Deposits, and Public Provident Fund.

### 👥 Demographic Overview
Analyze average investor age and understand the profile of the investor base.

### 🎯 Savings Objectives
Identify dominant financial goals such as Retirement Planning, Health Care, and Education.

### 📈 Gender-Based Analysis
Compare male and female investment preferences across different financial instruments.

### ⏳ Investment Duration
Explore preferred investment horizons from short-term to long-term commitments.

### 🔍 Monitoring Behavior
Understand how frequently investors monitor their investments (Monthly, Weekly, Daily).

### 📰 Information Sources
Analyze the most trusted sources influencing investment decisions.

### 💡 Investment Motivation
Evaluate key drivers such as Capital Appreciation, Assured Returns, Risk-Free returns, and Diversification.

---

## 📁 Dataset Description

The dataset is based on structured investor survey data and includes:

- Demographics: Age, Gender  
- Investment Preferences: Mutual Funds, Equity, Fixed Deposits, PPF  
- Savings Objectives: Retirement, Health Care, Education  
- Investment Duration Categories  
- Monitoring Frequency  
- Reasons for Investment  
- Sources of Investment Information  

---

## 🧠 Insights & Conclusions

- Mutual Funds account for *45%* of total preference, making them the most dominant investment option.
- The average investor age is *27.80 years*, indicating a young and growth-oriented population.
- Retirement Planning (*96 investors*) is the strongest financial objective.
- Most investors prefer a *1–5 year investment horizon, with **3–5 years* being the most common.
- Capital Appreciation is the leading investment motivation.
- Financial Consultants (*40%*) are the most trusted source of information.
- Male investors show relatively higher equity participation compared to female investors.
- Monthly monitoring is the most common investment tracking behavior.
